Drively App

This project was created among a team of developers, business professionals, and other designers. The goal was to create an application to aid food delivery drivers, specifically DoorDash employees at release, in their job. The core goals of the app were to allow social media style posts to inform drivers if for example a restaurant is very slow one day, allow drivers to rate restaurants so drivers will know which deliveries to accept/decline. We also decided on adding some other features like tracking your personal statistics and having a leaderboard system. I was chosen as the lead designer where I was responsible for coming up with the brand, as well as the mobile application. I also was responsible for overseeing and guiding two other designers who were tasked with creating the web application based on the branding and mobile application design.

Drively was ultimately short lived as the project was scrapped by the founder after about a month and a half of design and development.

The Branding Explained

When creating the brand for drively I was focused on a modern style to match the style of the different food delivery companies that our users work for. I was also concerned with having specific, recognizable elements that could be used in the applications as those are ultimately more important than the logo itself. I wanted to make sure users could look at the logo and the app and tell they are from the same company. The brand book explains all of these elements and was used to guide the designers I was overseeing in their design of the website application.
